## The Opportunity

The EU AI Act enforcement deadline is **August 2, 2026**. Every company deploying high-risk AI systems in the EU needs compliance evidence for Articles 11 (technical documentation), 12 (record-keeping), 13 (transparency), and 25 (value chain accountability).

The compliance tooling market is projected at $2.05B by 2030 (from $0.55B in 2025). Today, companies either hire consultants ($30K-$500K per engagement) or cobble together manual processes. No software product translates AI system traces into regulatory compliance evidence.

AI Trace Auditor fills that gap.

## What You're Buying

A production-quality Python CLI (v0.14.0) with 301 passing tests that:

1. **Ingests AI traces** from OpenTelemetry, Langfuse, Claude Code, or raw JSONL
2. **Audits against 6 regulatory frameworks:** EU AI Act Articles 11, 12, 13, 25; NIST AI RMF; GDPR Article 30
3. **Auto-generates Annex IV documentation** by scanning codebases for AI framework usage
4. **Maps data flows** with GDPR role classification (controller/processor/sub-processor)
5. **Audits multi-agent systems** (LangGraph, CrewAI, AutoGen, Google ADK) with per-agent penalty propagation and DAG visualization
6. **Runs in CI** via GitHub Action with pass/fail exit codes
7. **Produces evidence packs** (Markdown, JSON, PDF) that compliance officers hand to auditors

## Why This Can't Be Easily Replicated

1. **Regulatory domain expertise baked into code.** The YAML requirement mappings encode interpretations of EU AI Act articles, NIST subcategories, and GDPR obligations. This took months of legal research, not just engineering.

2. **Multi-agent compliance auditing.** No other tool (open-source or commercial) audits multi-agent AI systems against EU AI Act Article 25. DAG reconstruction, bottom-up penalty propagation, and per-agent scoring are novel.

3. **Guide linter catches its own mistakes.** Built-in linter validates that generated compliance guides correctly distinguish Article 13 vs 50, include scope checks, and use accurate retention periods.

4. **Time-to-market.** With the August 2026 deadline 4 months away, building from scratch means missing the initial compliance wave.
