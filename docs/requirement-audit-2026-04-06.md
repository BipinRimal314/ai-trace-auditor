## Methodology

Each requirement YAML was compared line-by-line against the primary legal text. Verdicts use three categories:

- **CORRECT**: Accurately reflects the law
- **WRONG SCOPE**: Based on real text but applied to wrong category of systems
- **FABRICATED**: Not in the cited article at all; projects modern LLM observability practices onto technology-neutral legal text

## Article 12 — Record-keeping (PDF page 59)

### What the law actually says

**12(1):** High-risk AI systems shall technically allow for the automatic recording of events (logs) over the lifetime of the system.

**12(2):** Logging shall enable recording events relevant for:
- (a) identifying situations that may result in risk per Art 79(1) or substantial modification
- (b) facilitating post-market monitoring per Art 72
- (c) monitoring the operation of high-risk AI systems per Art 26(5)

**12(3):** **Only for Annex III point 1(a) systems** (biometric identification), logging shall provide at minimum:
- (a) period of each use (start date+time, end date+time)
- (b) the reference database against which input data was checked
- (c) the input data for which the search led to a match
- (d) identification of natural persons involved in verification per Art 14(5)

### Requirement-by-requirement audit

| YAML ID | Title | Verdict | Detail |
|---------|-------|---------|--------|
| EU-AIA-12.1a | Event timestamp recording | **WRONG SCOPE** | Start/end timestamps are from 12(3)(a), which only applies to Annex III 1(a) biometric systems. YAML applies it to `["high_risk", "all"]`. |
| EU-AIA-12.1b | Operation identification | **FABRICATED** | Art 12 never mentions operation type, model name, or provider. These are LLM observability concepts, not legal requirements. |
| EU-AIA-12.2a | Risk identification logging | **PARTIALLY CORRECT** | Description paraphrases 12(2)(a) reasonably. But evidence_fields (error_type, error_message, finish_reasons) are invented; law doesn't specify implementation. Also drops "substantial modification" from 12(2)(a). |
| EU-AIA-12.2b | Model version tracking | **FABRICATED** | 12(2)(b) says "facilitating post-market monitoring per Art 72." It does not mention model versions, model_used, or model_requested. |
| EU-AIA-12.2c | Resource consumption monitoring | **FABRICATED** | Tokens, latency, and resource metrics are not in Article 12 at all. |
| EU-AIA-12.2d | Input/output content recording | **WRONG SCOPE** | 12(3)(c) mentions "input data for which the search led to a match" but only for biometric systems. YAML generalizes to all high-risk and adds output recording not in the law. |
| EU-AIA-12.2e | Tool and function call logging | **FABRICATED** | Not in Article 12. |
| EU-AIA-12.2f | Request parameter logging | **FABRICATED** | Temperature, top_p, max_tokens are not in Article 12. |
| EU-AIA-12.3 | Trace linkage and causality | **FABRICATED** | Span IDs, parent-child relationships, and trace linkage are not in Article 12. |

### Missing requirements from Article 12

| Law ref | Content | Status |
|---------|---------|--------|
| 12(2)(c) | Monitoring deployer operations per Art 26(5) | Not in YAML |
| 12(3)(b) | Reference database against which input was checked (biometric) | Not in YAML |
| 12(3)(d) | Identification of natural persons verifying results (biometric) | Not in YAML |

### Score: 1 partially correct, 2 wrong scope, 5 fabricated, 3 missing. Out of 9 YAML requirements, 0 are fully correct.

## Article 19 — Automatically generated logs (PDF page 64)

### What the law actually says

**19(1):** Providers shall keep logs from Art 12(1), automatically generated by their high-risk AI systems, to the extent such logs are under their control. Logs kept for period appropriate to intended purpose, at least six months, unless applicable law says otherwise.

**19(2):** Financial institution exception: logs maintained as part of financial services documentation.

## Article 25 — Responsibilities along the AI value chain (PDF page 67)

### What the law actually says

This article defines when a **distributor, importer, deployer, or other third party** becomes legally a "provider" and takes on provider obligations. It is a **legal liability** article, not a technical logging article.

**25(1):** A third party becomes a provider when:
- (a) they put their name/trademark on someone else's high-risk AI system
- (b) they make a substantial modification to a high-risk system already on the market
- (c) they modify the intended purpose so a non-high-risk system becomes high-risk

**25(2):** Original provider ceases to be provider when paragraph 1 applies; must cooperate with new provider.

**25(3):** For safety components in products, product manufacturer = provider.

**25(4):** Provider and third-party suppliers must specify by written agreement the necessary information, capabilities, and technical access.

**25(5):** Without prejudice to IP rights and trade secrets.

### Requirement-by-requirement audit

| YAML ID | Title | Verdict | Detail |
|---------|-------|---------|--------|
| EU-AIA-25.1 | Agent identity traceability | **COMPLETELY WRONG** | Art 25 is about legal liability, not AI agent identifiers. "Value chain" = provider→deployer→distributor, not AI-agent-to-AI-agent. |
| EU-AIA-25.2 | Delegation chain documentation | **COMPLETELY WRONG** | Conflates legal value chain with AI agent delegation. |
| EU-AIA-25.3 | MCP boundary transparency | **FABRICATED** | MCP is Anthropic's 2024 protocol. The EU AI Act does not mention it. |
| EU-AIA-25.4 | Substantial modification detection | **WRONG REFERENCE** | Cites "Article 25(1)(c)" but substantial modification is 25(1)(b). Also maps a legal concept to irrelevant technical fields. |

### Score: All 4 requirements are wrong. The entire YAML misinterprets what the article is about.

## Root Cause

The YAML files were written by projecting modern LLM observability best practices (OpenTelemetry spans, token counting, model version tracking, MCP protocol) onto EU AI Act articles that are intentionally technology-neutral. The law describes WHAT must be achievable (automatic event logging, risk traceability, retention) without prescribing HOW (specific fields, formats, protocols).

This is a category error: treating the EU AI Act as a technical specification when it is a legal framework.

## Remediation Plan

1. **Article 12:** Rewrite to separate general requirements (12(1), 12(2)) from biometric-specific (12(3)). Remove all fabricated field-level requirements. Where the tool checks specific fields, mark them as "implementation guidance" not "legal requirement."
2. **Article 19:** Remove fabricated 19.2. Add 19(2) financial provision.
3. **Article 25:** Complete rewrite. Capture the legal liability provisions as compliance checks (e.g., "has the deployer made a substantial modification?") rather than technical logging fields.
4. **Code audit:** Update any Python validators that reference removed fields.
5. **Labeling:** Distinguish between what the law requires ("mandatory per Article X") and what the tool recommends as best practice ("recommended implementation").

## NIST AI RMF — MEASURE and MANAGE (NIST AI 100-1, pp. 29-33)

### Framework nature

NIST AI RMF is a **voluntary** framework. Nothing in it is legally mandatory. Subcategory descriptions state organizational practices, not technical requirements.

### Findings

| YAML ID | Title | Description Accuracy | Evidence Fields | Issues |
|---------|-------|---------------------|-----------------|--------|
| NIST-MEASURE-2.1 | TEVV documentation | Reasonable paraphrase | `evaluations` (optional) | OK — description matches, field is guidance |
| NIST-MEASURE-2.4 | Production monitoring | Stretched — adds "degradation, drift" not in source | `model_used`, `input_tokens`, `output_tokens`, `duration_ms`, `finish_reasons` — ALL required | **5 fabricated required fields.** NIST says "monitored when in production" without specifying what to monitor. Severity was "mandatory" — wrong for a voluntary framework. |
| NIST-MEASURE-2.8 | Transparency documentation | Stretched — adds "must log sufficient data to explain" | `operation`, `provider`, `model_used` — all required | **3 fabricated required fields.** NIST says "examined and documented," not "logged in traces." |
| NIST-MEASURE-2.9 | Model explainability | Title narrower than actual; adds "must be logged" | `input_messages`, `output_messages`, `temperature` | Fields are optional — better than others. But still not NIST requirements. |
| NIST-MEASURE-3.1 | Risk tracking | Adds "Error tracking and anomaly detection must be in place" | `error_type` (required), `error_message`, `finish_reasons` (required) | **2 fabricated required fields.** NIST talks about organizational risk tracking approaches, not error logging. |
| NIST-MANAGE-4.1 | Post-deployment monitoring | Truncated — drops "appeal and override, decommissioning, incident response, recovery, and change management" | `start_time` (required), `model_used` (required), `duration_ms` | **Description distorted.** Key NIST provisions about appeal/override/decommissioning dropped. |

### Pattern

Subcategory IDs are real. Descriptions are stretched to justify trace-level checks. All evidence fields are fabricated. Severity labels were misleading (marking voluntary framework items as "mandatory").

## ISO 42001 — AI Management System (ISO/IEC 42001:2023)

### Framework nature

ISO 42001 is a **certifiable standard** following the Annex SL management system structure (clauses 4-10). Most clauses describe organizational/governance requirements.

### Structural problem

8 of 9 requirements map organizational clauses (leadership, context, resources, risk planning, audit) to trace-level fields. This is the same category error as EU AI Act Article 25: treating management system requirements as technical logging checks.

| Clause | Topic | Can trace inspection verify? | Original mapping |
|--------|-------|---------------------------|-----------------|
| 4.1 | Context of the organization | No | `provider` → absurd |
| 5.1 | Leadership commitment | No | `operation` → absurd |
| 6.1 | Risk assessment | No | `error_type`, `finish_reasons` → wrong level |
| 7.1 | Resources | No | `input_tokens`, `output_tokens` → "resources" ≠ tokens |
| 8.1 | Operational planning | Partially | `model_used`, `start_time` → reasonable proxy |
| 8.4 | Impact assessment | No | `provider`, `operation` → wrong level |
| 9.1 | Monitoring and measurement | Partially | `latency_ms`, timestamps → reasonable proxy |
| 9.2 | Internal audit | Partially | `trace_id` → weak but defensible |
| 10.1 | Continual improvement | No | `model_used`, `error_type` → wrong level |

### Unverified

Full text verification requires purchased standard. Clause numbers and titles follow known Annex SL structure and are likely accurate. Descriptions are plausible paraphrases.
